Email addresses explained


1. An email address is similar to your home address, it lets others communicate with you by sending messages to your virtual/online address.

2. An email address consists of two parts, a person's chosen username and the website that manages their email.

They are formatted as username@website, and this is always the same.

3. The first part, the username, is free for you to choose, many people use their first or last names, initials, and birthdate in some combination to try and create a unique identity. Others create usernames that remind them of things they like.

For example, a boy named Marco Farrugia created a couple of the following names:

Mfarrugi, his first initial and most of his last name.
A.F.Marco, his middle and last initial, followed by his first name.
Marco, just his first name (this is often already claimed!)
Marco1998, his name with his birth year at the end, since 'Marco' was already in use.

4. The second part, the host name, is the name of the website that manages your email, acting as a digital version of the postal service that would deliver normal mail.

The host name comes from which website you decide to use to track your emails, and the naming portion doesn't mean much beyond that. But the different services offer slightly different features, kind of like how USPS and Fedex aren't exactly the same.

To continue the example, Marco's username Mfarrugi would look like:

Mfarrugi@gmail.com - Gmail is Google's web mail offering. Mfarrugi@msn.com - Microsoft's web mail hosting. Mfarrugi@mit.edu - MIT's mail hosting.

Each of the above examples is a valid email address, following the username@website format.
